# Audit 001 — inaugural pass

## Scope

All five council agents (architecture-reviewer, qa-reviewer, typescript-reviewer, security-reviewer, writer) at the state set up by Phase 1 + Phase 2 commits, plus the historical-evolution review entries from Phase 3 (iter 1→2, iter 2→3, iter 7→8). Codebase verified at worktree HEAD. Source-of-truth design doc: project root `CLAUDE.md`. Date range: all entries dated 2026-04-25.

## Findings

### Cross-agent contradictions

**1. `AutomatonLike` — architect vs typescript-reviewer (active disagreement, productively preserved).**

- architecture-reviewer/journal/2026-04-25-historical-iter7-iter8-review.md "Note on the AutomatonLike structural shadow": calls it "borderline-acceptable" and labels this a "**partial revision** of my prior position," referencing a "concrete caller" and a "would lose type info under a union" defense.
- typescript-reviewer/journal/2026-04-25-historical-iter7-iter8-review.md: explicitly disagrees, claims the cast was present at the introducing commit (`1493832`), counts a *second* cast at the App.tsx caller, asserts "no engine import in UI" was never a real motivation (UI may import engine), and reinforces the prior verdict.
- typescript-reviewer/knowledge/generic-constraints.md was updated with the reinforced position; architecture-reviewer/knowledge/engine-ui-separation.md still describes `AutomatonLike` as "one known crack" — i.e., the two **knowledge files are now consistent** in describing it as a defect, but the architect's **journal entry is the outlier**, holding a softer verdict than its own knowledge base.

   *They are not talking past each other.* They disagree on a verifiable claim ("born clean" vs "born with cast"). I verified: at HEAD, `creationReducer.ts:437` and `:568` both contain `as unknown as T`. Two casts. typescript-reviewer's count and verdict align with the code; architect's journal is in tension with both their own knowledge file and the code.

**2. `getOrphanedStates` vs `hasOrphanedStates` — CLAUDE.md drift, multiple agents.**

CLAUDE.md "Validation Strategy" lists `hasOrphanedStates()`. Actual symbol is `getOrphanedStates` (engine/validator.ts:188), exported and tested. No agent has flagged this. Architecture-reviewer's `engine-ui-separation.md` and `state-id-management.md` both cite CLAUDE.md as provenance without noticing the drift. Low impact, but it's exactly the class of staleness the auditor is meant to catch.

### Internal contradictions

**3. architecture-reviewer: "borderline-acceptable" (journal) vs "one known crack" (knowledge index + engine-ui-separation.md).**

The agent's own knowledge file `engine-ui-separation.md` (verified-as-of `52bdb8e`, confidence: high) says: "One known crack: ... `AutomatonLike` ... results in `as unknown as T` casts." The iter 7→8 journal entry softens this to "borderline-acceptable, not premature abstraction." The two cannot both be the agent's current position. Either the knowledge file should be updated or the journal verdict should be withdrawn.

**4. qa-reviewer: synthetic-verification correction did not propagate to knowledge.**

qa-reviewer/journal/2026-04-25-synthetic-verification.md ends with an "Orchestrator correction note" admitting the agent mischaracterized `startState` as `number | null` when it is actually `number` (non-nullable, types.ts:79 — verified). The journal explicitly queues a knowledge update: "add a knowledge file or expand `test-coverage-map.md` with current data-shape facts ... The auditor should pick this up."

That update was never made. None of qa-reviewer/knowledge/*.md mentions `startState` nullability or the `hasStartState` invariant. The latent mistake is still uncorrected in the agent's persistent layer; the next time qa-reviewer reasons about `startState` semantics from knowledge alone, it will recreate the error.

This is exactly the "agent reinforces own past judgments without re-examining them" pattern the role description anticipates — except in this case it's the *correction* that wasn't propagated, not a wrong judgment.

### Stale knowledge

**5. typescript-reviewer/knowledge/cast-discipline.md and generic-constraints.md cite stale line numbers.**

Both files say the `as unknown as T` casts in `creationReducer.ts` are at "lines 415, 494, 548" — verified-as-of `52bdb8e`. At HEAD, the actual locations are `:437` and `:568`. **Two casts, not three.** Either way, citations need to be re-verified.

**6. qa-reviewer/knowledge/test-coverage-map.md cites `computePreview` conflict branches at "lines 416-444 and 519-541."**

Same source file (creationReducer.ts), same staleness risk. File grew from ~280 → 629 lines across iter 7→8. I did not verify these specific ranges, but given the typescript-reviewer's stale citations on the same file, both gap-doc line ranges should be re-verified before being cited in the next live review.

**7. architecture-reviewer/knowledge/external-dependency-boundary.md states `utils.ts` is "~480 lines."**

Actual: 478. Effectively accurate; flagged only because the gap entry (#6) explicitly anticipates "when `src/ui-state/utils.ts` grows past ~600 lines and natural splitting points emerge" as a trigger to revisit the open question. The file is not close to 600.

### Confidence-evidence mismatches

**8. All five architecture-reviewer knowledge files are marked `confidence: high`, but the foundational one (engine-ui-separation) is in active tension with that agent's own iter 7→8 journal verdict.**

A knowledge file holding a verdict the agent's journal has softened is, definitionally, not high-confidence. Either confidence should be downgraded, or the journal verdict should be reconciled (see finding #3).

**9. qa-reviewer/knowledge/test-patterns.md asserts "no `test()` aliases; consistently `describe`/`it`" with `confidence: high`.**

I did not verify this against the codebase. Plausible, but it's a claim about a global property of N test files made from a sample of 5 (per the provenance line). High confidence on a universal claim from a non-exhaustive sample is the smell the reviewer-disposition warns about elsewhere.

### Drift from CLAUDE.md

**10. CLAUDE.md says `hasOrphanedStates()`; code says `getOrphanedStates`.** (See finding #2.)

**11. CLAUDE.md says iter 3 is "(PLANNED)"; code reality is iter 9+10 merged.**

The CLAUDE.md "Iteration 3: Advanced Visualization (PLANNED)" header has not been updated to reflect that iter 3 (and 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10) are all now complete. The "Current Status" line at the top of CLAUDE.md *does* say iter 9+10 merged, so the doc is internally inconsistent. None of the council agents flag this, but architecture-reviewer cites CLAUDE.md as provenance for several claims; the agent's stance toward its own source-of-truth doc may need to include "and check that the doc itself is current." This is a council-level observation, not a single-agent fault.

**12. CLAUDE.md lists `Iteration 6: NFA Support` as a future iteration (not complete), but the iter 7→8 review confirms NFA support shipped.**

Same drift as #11. CLAUDE.md's "Future Iterations (Backlog)" section is no longer accurate post-iter-8.

### Patterns observed (meta)

**P1 — Synthetic-verification entries are doing useful disconfirmation work, but the corrections aren't propagating to knowledge.**

All four non-writer agents have synthetic-verification journal entries. Three are clean (architect, ts, security: each found violations matching existing knowledge, validated their knowledge base scope). One — qa-reviewer's — surfaced a real mistake the agent made (the `startState` nullability error) and the correction was queued but never landed in knowledge. **The journal layer is being used for self-criticism in the right way; the knowledge layer is not absorbing those corrections. This is the most important pattern to fix structurally before iteration 11 starts producing real diffs.**

**P2 — Architect-vs-types disagreement is healthy and should not be auto-resolved.**

The `AutomatonLike` disagreement (finding #1) is a feature, not a bug. Both agents have evidence-backed positions; the typescript-reviewer's is stronger on the type-level facts; the architect's softer verdict appears to come from "concrete caller exists, abstraction is localized" reasoning that's plausible at the design level even if the cast-count argument is wrong. The orchestrator should preserve the disagreement for the project owner to resolve, not pick a winner. *But the architect should reconcile their journal verdict against their own knowledge file* — that internal contradiction (#3) is separable from the cross-agent one and should be closed.

**P3 — Line-number citations are fragile and already drifting after one iteration of growth.**

Findings #5 and #6 both involve stale file-position citations after a single round of merges. The convention going forward should be: cite by symbol name + file, not line number, except in audit-style entries that explicitly snapshot a commit. Recommend a convention update in `_conventions.md` (orchestrator-level decision; flagged here as a meta-observation).

**P4 — CLAUDE.md is treated as immutable source-of-truth but is itself drifting.**

Findings #10–#12 show the project's own design doc has lagged the code. Agents that cite CLAUDE.md as provenance are inheriting that drift. The council needs a story for "what does it mean for an agent's high-confidence knowledge to cite a doc that has fallen behind the code?" — currently it means "high confidence in a stale claim."

## Recommended actions

### architecture-reviewer
- **Reconcile the `AutomatonLike` verdict.** Either update `engine-ui-separation.md` to match the journal's softer "borderline-acceptable" framing, or withdraw the journal's "partial revision" and keep the knowledge file's "one known crack" verdict. Internal consistency required either way. (Findings #1, #3, #8.)
- **Re-verify the cast-count claim against `creationReducer.ts` HEAD.** Architect's knowledge file's "one known crack ... results in `as unknown as T` casts" should add a count and a re-verified-as-of bump.
- **Flag CLAUDE.md drift as an open question.** The `hasOrphanedStates`/`getOrphanedStates` mismatch and the stale "PLANNED" / "Future" iteration sections should be raised — this affects every knowledge file that cites CLAUDE.md as provenance.

### qa-reviewer
- **Propagate the `startState` correction into knowledge.** The synthetic-verification journal explicitly queued this; it never landed. Add a knowledge file (or expand `test-coverage-map.md`) recording: `startState: number` (non-nullable, since iter-1 review); the `hasStartState` validator invariant in validator.ts:117 protects against stale-ID-after-removal cases. (Finding #4, pattern P1.)
- **Re-verify the `computePreview` conflict-branch line ranges in `known-coverage-gaps.md` and `test-coverage-map.md`.** File grew significantly post-iter-8; cite by function name + branch description rather than line number. (Finding #6.)

### typescript-reviewer
- **Update line numbers in `cast-discipline.md` and `generic-constraints.md`.** Verified actual locations at HEAD: `creationReducer.ts:437` and `:568`. The "three points / lines 415, 494, 548" claim is stale. Recommend switching to symbol-relative citations. (Finding #5, pattern P3.)
- **Position on `AutomatonLike` is well-supported; no change requested.** The disagreement with the architect is healthy and should remain.

### security-reviewer
- **No findings.** Both knowledge files are accurate as of HEAD; the synthetic-verification journal is well-scoped and the conditional-update plan is the right stance. Continue.

### writer
- **No findings.** Identity-only by design; correctly has no persistent state to audit.

### Orchestrator / council-level
- **Convention update**: add a guideline to `_conventions.md` to prefer symbol-relative citations over line numbers in knowledge files. Reserve line numbers for audit/journal entries that explicitly snapshot a commit. (Pattern P3.)
- **CLAUDE.md drift**: surface findings #10–#12 to the project owner. These are not within any single agent's authority to fix — CLAUDE.md is the owner's doc — but the council's reliance on it as provenance means the owner should know it has drifted. (Pattern P4.)

## Closing notes

This is the inaugural audit. Baseline observations:

- Knowledge-file quality is generally high. All five non-writer agents have well-scoped, evidence-cited knowledge bases. The defects found are corrections-not-propagated and citations-going-stale, not fundamental misunderstandings.
- The historical-review entries (iter 1→2, 2→3, 7→8) are the richest material in the council and the most useful for cross-agent auditing. They should keep being produced when major iterations land.
- The synthetic-verification entries did their job: they revealed scope-coverage of the knowledge bases (good) and surfaced one real mistake (the qa `startState` error) that is the most important single fix coming out of this audit.
- The most important *structural* fix is closing the loop between journal corrections and knowledge updates (pattern P1). Without that, the agents' corrections are write-only — visible to me as auditor, invisible to the agents themselves on their next read.
